# big-history-lab

Visual history of ideas & innovation. 9 self-contained interactive demos for the IE BCSAI course *The Big History of Ideas and Innovation* (Al-Marashi; readings from Headrick's *Technology: A World History*, Harari, Tegmark, McLuhan).

**Live:** https://andreaisabelmontana.github.io/big-history-lab/

Part 1 — Background & deep time
1. Eight thresholds — click through Big History's eight moments of rising complexity (Big Bang → modernity) on a logarithmic time axis
2. Cosmic clock — scrub 13.8 Bya to today and watch all of human history compress into the last seconds of the Cosmic Calendar (linear/log toggle)

Part 2 — Curves of innovation
3. Diffusion of innovation — logistic adoption S-curves for the telephone, radio, TV, mobile and smartphone; tune steepness `k`
4. Accelerating returns — exponential vs linear growth with a log/linear axis toggle (Moore's-law-style doubling, Kurzweil)
5. World population — curated estimates from 10,000 BCE to today, with the innovations that drove each jump (log/linear toggle)

Part 3 — Ideas, complexity & the digital future
6. Idea map — clickable genealogy from writing to AI; highlights what each idea builds on and what it enables
7. Rising complexity — the "Goldilocks conditions" window where complexity can survive (too cold / too hot / just right)
8. Revolutions timeline — pan & zoom through turning points from the Stone Age to ChatGPT; events bunch toward the present
9. Collective learning — how shared knowledge compounds across generations with more minds and better media (oral → writing → print → internet)

Plain HTML + canvas + KaTeX. Indigo accent. Zero build step, zero dependencies.
